Academic AI Policy

A comprehensive framework for the academic use of generative AI in university teaching and learning. It defines principles, allowed practices, declaration requirements, and evaluation mechanisms.

  • Summary: AI is a cognitive partner, not a replacement. Students declare AI use, show substantial modification, and reflect critically.
  • Languages: English · Українська

RAID-G — Responsible AI Declaration Generator

A web tool to create structured AI-use declarations aligned with the policy. Features an easy form, dropdowns with custom values, quick-insert chips (Refactoring, Boilerplate, Tests), and bilingual output.

AI Teaching Assistant (in development)

An educational assistant focused on learning: supports fair evaluation, suggests code refactoring, explains theory, and guides students to build skills—instead of just providing final answers.
